Situated just a short walk away from the seafront and the South Downs at the centre of Meads Village in Eastbourne, this pretty Victorian fisherman’s cottage is an ideal escape for couples and families. The Cottage has been thoughtfully restored to provide a contemporary accommodation, whilst in keeping with the property’s Victorian roots.

Downstairs, there is a lounge with comfy sofas and a TV, which overlooks the gardens. There is also a well equipped kitchen/diner with a good sized dining table, an electric oven, hob and a microwave. From the kitchen there is a door to the patio garden, with a washing machine in the outbuilding. Upstairs, there is a double bedroom with views over the gardens, which benefits from an en-suite shower room, as well as a twin bedroom and a small single bedroom. Also on this floor, guests can find the large bathroom which offers a shower over bath and WC.


Outside, there is a secluded courtyard garden with table and chairs, an ideal spot for lunch or an evening drink after a day of exploring the beautiful Sussex countryside. At the front of the cottage, there is the outlook over the square, with private allotments and flower gardens. You can unload your car outside the property and then there is free roadside parking nearby.


Dogs are charged at £20 each per full or part week.

The Cottage is located just a few minutes walk away from the Holywell beach. The beach at this side of town is quieter than Eastbourne's main beach and is backed on one side by pretty italian-style gardens. There is a thriving community in Meads Village, which offers a good choice of shops, restaurants and pubs. Meads Village is also on the edge of the South Downs national park, with Beachy Head being less than 2 miles away.


Eastbourne town centre, which is well server by local buses, is just two miles away and offers a traditional beach with beach hut hire, the lovingly restored Victorian pier and seaside shops.
